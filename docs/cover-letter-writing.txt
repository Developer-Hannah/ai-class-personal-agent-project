OVERVIEW
A cover letter is a focused pitch that explains why your background matches a specific role. It can highlight strengths that are not obvious from resume bullets alone.

WHEN TO INCLUDE ONE
- Required if the posting asks for a cover letter.
- Usually helpful even when marked optional.
- If you email a resume directly, the email body can often serve as the letter.

RECOMMENDED LENGTH AND STRUCTURE
Keep the letter concise, generally around 200 to 400 words in about three to four short paragraphs.

Suggested sections:
1. Heading and greeting with contact details and a named recipient when possible.
2. Intro paragraph stating role interest and context.
3. Body paragraphs connecting your skills and achievements to employer needs.
4. Closing paragraph thanking the reader and signaling follow-up interest.

CUSTOMIZATION PRINCIPLES
- Tailor each letter to one employer and one role.
- Reference specific qualifications from the posting.
- Explain how your experience can contribute to the organization.
- Keep tone professional, energetic, and specific.

PRACTICAL TIPS
- Avoid generic greetings when a specific person or role title is available.
- Reuse a template structure, but rewrite content for each application.
- Review carefully for errors before sending.
- If AI is used for drafting, edit heavily so the final result reflects your real voice and facts.
