Cover Letter Writing Guide

A great cover letter is not a summary of your resume — it is a targeted pitch that explains why you specifically are the right person for this specific role at this specific company. It bridges the gap between your resume and the job description.

WHEN TO WRITE ONE
Always submit a cover letter unless the application explicitly says not to. Even when listed as optional, a compelling letter differentiates you. Hiring managers who read cover letters are often the ones making final decisions.

STRUCTURE
Keep it to three to four paragraphs, fitting on one page. Use a standard business letter format.

Opening paragraph: Hook them immediately. Lead with the most compelling reason you are a strong candidate, or with a specific connection to the company. Avoid "I am applying for the position of..." — that is obvious.

Example: "When I saw that Acme Corp is expanding into European markets, I immediately thought of the two years I spent building international payment systems at FinTech Co — scaling from 3 to 14 currencies while maintaining 99.9% uptime."

Middle paragraph(s): Connect your experience directly to the top two or three requirements in the job description. Use specific examples with results, not generic claims. If the job description says "cross-functional leadership," describe a specific time you led cross-functional work and what it produced.

Closing paragraph: Express genuine enthusiasm for the company and role. Reference something specific about the company — a product, mission statement, or recent development — that resonates with you. Include a clear call to action: "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background aligns with what you are building."

TAILORING IS EVERYTHING
Never send a generic cover letter. For each application:
1. Re-read the job description and underline the top 3 requirements
2. Match each requirement to a specific achievement from your background
3. Research the company and reference something specific
4. Mirror the language and tone from the job posting

A tailored cover letter takes 30–45 minutes but substantially increases your callback rate.

TONE AND VOICE
Write in a confident, professional, but conversational tone. Avoid stiff corporate language. Read your letter out loud — if it sounds like something a robot wrote, rewrite it. Use "I" freely. Show personality without being informal.

COMMON MISTAKES
- Restating your entire resume in paragraph form
- Starting every sentence with "I"
- Focusing on what you want, not what you offer
- Generic praise: "I have always admired your company's commitment to innovation"
- Typos, grammar errors, or getting the company name wrong
- Exceeding one page

LENGTH AND FORMAT
Three to four focused paragraphs is ideal. Never exceed one page. Use the same font and header style as your resume for a cohesive application package. Save as PDF unless the employer specifies otherwise.

ADDRESSING THE LETTER
Use "Dear [Hiring Manager's Name]" if you can find it. Research LinkedIn or the company website. If you genuinely cannot find a name, use "Dear Hiring Team" — not "To Whom It May Concern."
