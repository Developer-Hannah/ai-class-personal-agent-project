OVERVIEW
Your resume should help employers quickly confirm that you meet minimum qualifications. Reviewers often scan very fast, so key qualifications and role-relevant terms should appear early and clearly.

CORE GUIDELINES
- Keep formatting simple and easy to read.
- Keep length focused, usually one to two pages.
- Use language that matches the role you are targeting.
- Prioritize evidence that aligns with the posting.

WHAT TO INCLUDE
- Contact details employers can use immediately.
- A targeted summary or highlights section when useful.
- Work history with concrete accomplishments.
- Technical or professional skills relevant to the job.
- Education and credentials needed for the role.

TARGETING FOR ATS AND HUMAN READERS
Many employers use applicant tracking systems (ATS). That means your resume should include exact keywords from job postings where they truthfully apply to your experience. A strong approach is to scan multiple postings for the same role and identify recurring requirements.

QUALITY CHECK BEFORE SUBMITTING
- Proofread for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
- Confirm details are accurate and truthful.
- Ensure file format and submission instructions match the posting.
- Ask another person to review readability and relevance.

USING AI TO ASSIST
AI tools can help brainstorm wording, suggest alternatives, or compare your resume against a posting. Final editing should still be done by you so the document stays accurate, authentic, and specific to your background.
