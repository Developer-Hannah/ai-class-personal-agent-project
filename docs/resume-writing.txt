Resume Writing Tips for Job Seekers

A strong resume is your first impression with a potential employer. It must be clear, concise, and tailored to the specific role you are applying for.

FORMAT AND LENGTH
Keep your resume to one page if you have less than ten years of experience. Two pages is acceptable for senior professionals. Use a clean, readable font like Calibri, Arial, or Georgia at 10–12pt. Leave adequate white space — a cluttered resume is hard to scan.

CONTACT INFORMATION
Put your name prominently at the top. Include your city and state (not full address), professional email address, LinkedIn URL, and phone number. A GitHub profile or portfolio link is valuable for technical roles.

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
Open with a 2–3 sentence summary that highlights your most relevant experience and what you bring to the role. Tailor this section for every application. Avoid generic phrases like "hard worker" or "team player."

WORK EXPERIENCE
List positions in reverse chronological order. For each role include: job title, company name, location, and dates (month/year). Use bullet points starting with strong action verbs: led, built, reduced, increased, designed, managed. Quantify achievements wherever possible — "Reduced deployment time by 40%" is stronger than "Improved deployment process."

SKILLS SECTION
List technical skills, tools, and programming languages relevant to the role. Keep it honest — you should be able to answer interview questions on anything listed. Group skills logically: Languages, Frameworks, Tools, Platforms.

EDUCATION
List degree, major, university name, graduation year. Include GPA only if above 3.5. Relevant coursework, honors, and extracurricular activities can be included for recent graduates.

TAILORING YOUR RESUME
Read the job description carefully and mirror the language used. If the posting says "cross-functional collaboration," use that phrase if it accurately describes your experience. Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan for keyword matches before a human ever reads your resume.

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
- Using a generic resume for every application
- Including an "Objective" statement (use a Summary instead)
- Listing job duties instead of accomplishments
- Spelling or grammar errors (proofread at least three times)
- Using personal pronouns ("I," "my")
- Including references or "References available upon request"

ACTION VERBS TO USE
Achieved, Analyzed, Built, Coordinated, Delivered, Designed, Developed, Directed, Drove, Established, Executed, Generated, Implemented, Improved, Increased, Launched, Led, Managed, Negotiated, Optimized, Produced, Reduced, Streamlined, Transformed.
