OVERVIEW
Networking is a structured part of job search, not an optional extra. A practical strategy is to define your message, update your online presence, and contact people who can provide information or introductions.

STEP 1: BUILD A SHORT NETWORKING MESSAGE
Prepare a concise "elevator speech" that includes:
- your target role or direction,
- your strongest relevant skills/experience,
- and the specific help you are requesting (for example, contacts, advice, or referrals).

STEP 2: PREPARE YOUR ONLINE PRESENCE
Update social and professional profiles before outreach so contacts can quickly understand your background and goals.

STEP 3: MAP CONTACTS
Make a list of people who may know your target field directly or indirectly. A small, focused list (for example 3 to 4 people to start) is enough to begin.

STEP 4: REACH OUT PROFESSIONALLY
Contact people through email, social platforms, or phone using a concise message. Thank them for their time, and follow up on recommendations they provide.

NETWORKING STYLE THAT WORKS
- Be clear and specific about your goal.
- Keep requests lightweight and respectful.
- Follow through on leads promptly.
- Maintain professionalism and gratitude in every interaction.
