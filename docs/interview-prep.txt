OVERVIEW
Interviews are both an evaluation and a two-way fit check. Good preparation improves confidence and helps you provide clear, relevant examples.

BEFORE THE INTERVIEW
- Practice common questions out loud.
- Prepare short stories that show strengths, learning ability, flexibility, leadership, and problem solving.
- Compare each job requirement with your experience so you can address gaps directly.
- Prepare thoughtful questions about the role, team, tools, and company direction.

DAY-OF EXECUTION
- Arrive about 10 to 15 minutes early.
- Dress in a way that fits the role and workplace.
- Bring notes and materials you may need.
- Silence your phone and remove distractions.

COMMUNICATION DURING THE INTERVIEW
- Let the interviewer lead pacing, but answer with confidence.
- Keep body language positive and attentive.
- Listen fully before answering and ask for clarification when needed.
- Give honest, specific responses that connect your background to job needs.

HOW TO CLOSE STRONGLY
- Re-emphasize key strengths relevant to the role.
- Confirm your interest if you want the position.
- Ask about timeline and next steps.
- Send a short thank-you note after the interview.
