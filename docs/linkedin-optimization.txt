OVERVIEW
Social media can improve job search outcomes by helping candidates research employers, build industry connections, and present a professional profile. Employers may review public profiles during hiring.

HOW TO USE PLATFORMS EFFECTIVELY
- Follow target employers to track openings and company priorities.
- Connect with professionals, recruiters, and relevant employees.
- Participate in industry groups and discussions.
- Share useful projects, insights, or field-relevant content.
- Use portfolio links or work samples to strengthen credibility.

PROFILE STANDARDS
- Use a recent professional photo.
- Keep profile details current and aligned with target roles.
- Highlight skills and experience with searchable keywords.
- Review privacy settings and public visibility.
- Remove content that could hurt professional perception.
- Proofread posts and profile text for grammar and clarity.

LINKEDIN-SPECIFIC NOTES
Career-focused platforms such as LinkedIn are especially important for keyword visibility, professional networking, and recruiter discovery. Keep headlines, skills, and role descriptions aligned with the types of jobs you are pursuing.

SAFETY AND PROFESSIONALISM
Assume employers may view what is public. Only publish content, comments, and media you would be comfortable discussing in an interview.
