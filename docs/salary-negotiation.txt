OVERVIEW
Salary discussion is best handled after a formal offer. Taking time to review the full package before accepting can improve long-term outcomes.

WHEN TO TALK PAY
- Wait until an offer is made before negotiating salary.
- Thank the employer and request time (at least a day) to review details.
- Evaluate the entire package, not only base salary.

WHAT TO REVIEW IN AN OFFER
- Role scope, reporting structure, and schedule expectations.
- Base pay and major benefits (insurance, retirement, leave, flexibility).
- Growth factors such as training, advancement, and education support.
- Personal fit factors such as commute, culture, and household needs.

NEGOTIATION APPROACH
- Keep communication positive and professional.
- Base requests on value, qualifications, and market range.
- Use local pay benchmarks to set realistic targets.
- If salary flexibility is limited, ask about other negotiable items.

MARKET DATA USAGE
Use wage data tools and role-specific local ranges to judge whether an offer is below, within, or above typical compensation for that occupation and region.

DECISION POINTS
If the offer still does not align after discussion, choose whether to accept, request specific adjustments, or decline respectfully and keep the relationship positive.
